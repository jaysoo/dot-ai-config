## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (High Priority)

1. **Evaluate `enquirer` replacement** -- Used in nx core, devkit, vite, and angular. Last published July 2023. Consider `@inquirer/prompts` (actively maintained, similar API) or `@clack/prompts` (modern, good DX). This is the highest-impact unmaintained dependency.

2. **Audit `loader-utils@2.x`** in @nx/webpack -- Historical CVEs in v2. Verify the installed version is >=2.0.4 (patched). Consider migrating to v3 or removing if usage is minimal.

3. **Upgrade `parse5` from 4.0.0 to 7.x/8.x** in @nx/webpack -- 4 major versions behind. parse5 v4 is from 2018 and may have unfixed issues.

### Short-term Actions (Next Quarter)

4. **Remove `source-map-support`** from @nx/js -- Node.js has built-in source map support via `--enable-source-maps`. This 33-month-stale dependency may be unnecessary.

5. **Upgrade `fork-ts-checker-webpack-plugin`** from 7.x to 9.x in @nx/webpack -- 2 major versions behind, and the latest has better performance.

6. **Upgrade `copy-webpack-plugin`** from ^10 to ^13 in @nx/webpack and @nx/next.

7. **Evaluate ESM-only upgrades** -- `figures` (3->6), `flat` (5->6), `open` (8->10), `npm-run-path` (4->6), and `cli-cursor` (3->5) are all behind due to ESM-only migrations. As Nx moves toward ESM, these can be upgraded.

### Low Priority (Monitor)

8. **`tree-kill`**, **`node-machine-id`**, **`front-matter`**, **`columnify`**, **`lines-and-columns`** -- These are small, stable, "done" utilities. No action needed unless security issues arise.

9. **`identity-obj-proxy`** in @nx/jest -- Stable CSS mock for Jest. No functional issues, but if Jest's native mock capabilities improve, this can be removed.

10. **`babel-plugin-const-enum`** and **`babel-plugin-macros`** in @nx/js -- Niche Babel plugins. Monitor but no immediate action needed unless Babel drops compatibility.

### Ecosystem Health Notes

- The core build tooling (webpack, vite, babel, typescript, eslint) is all actively maintained with releases in the last 2 weeks.
- Angular ecosystem dependencies are on a healthy release cadence.
- The Jest ecosystem packages (@jest/reporters, jest-config, etc.) were last published Sep 2025 -- Jest 30 is likely in development.
- SWC ecosystem (@swc/core) is very active with daily releases.
